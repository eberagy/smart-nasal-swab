# Competitive Landscape Report (2024–2026)

## 1. Direct competitors — at-home / POC nasal & respiratory diagnostics

The category is a graveyard with a few aggressive survivors. **Cue Health** (Chapter
7, May 2024, after a $2.3B IPO peak; FDA revoked its EUA over unauthorized device
modifications) and **Lucira/Pfizer** (Pfizer bought Lucira out of bankruptcy in 2023,
killed the diagnostic division in March 2025, EUAs revoked Oct 2025) and **Ellume**
(liquidated 2023 after a Class I recall of 2.2M tests for false positives) have all
collapsed. **LumiraDx** went into UK pre-pack administration; Roche bought its POC
platform for ~$353M (closed July 2024) — so its tech survives, but inside Roche.

The survivors are clinic/POC-shaped, not consumer-shaped. **Visby Medical** got a
510(k) + CLIA waiver for its handheld single-use PCR Flu A/B + COVID test in Feb
2025, raised >$135M Series E (BARDA-backed, with at-home version in development).
**Aptitude Medical's Metrix** got an EUA for an OTC at-home + POC Flu/COVID multiplex
in Feb 2025 — currently the cleanest "true at-home molecular multiplex" on market.
**bioMérieux's BIOFIRE SPOTFIRE R/ST Mini** is the syndromic POC leader (anterior
nasal swab cleared Q3 2025; >200K patients tested through WellStreet alone).
**Detect** has gone quiet since 2022 — no 2024–2026 funding or product news.
**QuidelOrtho/Sofia** and **Abbott BinaxNOW/ID NOW** remain the volume incumbents but
are antigen-only and dumb (no AI). **Truvian (TruVerus)** got 510(k) in Dec 2025 for
a multimodal blood (not nasal) benchtop. **Genalyte's Merlin** targets late-2025
launch with 37-assay POC panel — also blood, not nasal.

## 2. CRISPR-based POC startups

**Sherlock Biosciences** has an OTC disposable molecular STI test (CT/NG) in clinical
trials (PROMISE study, 2024–2025) but no at-home respiratory product shipping.
**Mammoth Biosciences** essentially exited diagnostics in 2024–2025 — pivoted to
therapeutics with a $100M Regeneron deal (April 2024), did a 13% layoff in May 2025,
licensed CRISPR diagnostic IP to VedaBio (Sept 2025). **Caspr Biotech** remains
pre-commercial. Net: CRISPR-at-home for respiratory pathogens is *vapor* in 2026.

## 3. AI/ML + diagnostics

Almost no one is doing **AI on test-result interpretation** for at-home pathogen
tests. The AI healthcare gold rush is in adjacent lanes: **OpenEvidence** (760K
registered US physicians, 18M consults/month — clinical literature search, not test
reading), **Glass Health** (ambient scribing + differential diagnosis), **Hippocratic
AI** (patient-facing voice agents, non-diagnostic). **Quest's "AI Companion"**
(Gemini-powered, 2025) is the closest analog — chat over your existing lab results,
but lab-shaped not POC-shaped. **PathAI/Paige** are pathology-imaging only. **K
Health** is symptom-checker + telehealth. **Babylon** collapsed in 2023. *No one is
gluing pathogen ID + antibody titer + treatment recommendation into a single
consumer-facing AI.*

## 4. Antibody / immune-status

**Adaptive Biotechnologies' T-Detect** uses TCR sequencing + Microsoft AI for COVID
and Lyme — but it's a blood draw, sent to a central lab, weeks-long, never went
mucosal. **Serimmune** was acquired by Infinity Bio in June 2025 ($8M Series A from
Illumina Ventures); still serum-based, still lab-based. **Karius** raised $100M
Series C (May 2024, Khosla/5AM/Gilde-led), has FDA Breakthrough Designation, and
launched Karius Focus|BAL (April 2025) — but it's plasma cfDNA on bronchoalveolar
lavage, hospital-only. **Nobody has a shipping mucosal-IgA at-home product.**
Academic literature confirms IgA dominates early SARS-CoV-2 response and is
collectible from nasal/saliva — the science is there, the product is not.

## 5. Smart home-test readers (CV / phone-camera)

**Healthy.io** is the regulatory blueprint: Class II 510(k) for smartphone urinalysis
(2018), home kidney ACR test cleared 2022; UK NHS scaled to 540K patients. They
proved the FDA path. **Scanwell + Lemonaid** + **BD Veritor** combined CV-reader +
telehealth Rx for UTI and COVID (BD got first smartphone-read at-home COVID EUA, Aug
2021). **Cue's reader** died with the company. The pattern: 510(k) Class II,
color-board calibration, lock the phone-camera pipeline for accuracy.

## 6. Failures & lessons

**Theranos** — fabricated tech. **Ellume** — manufacturing QA collapse → Class I
recall → liquidation. **Cue Health** — single-product, COVID-only, unauthorized
device modifications, post-pandemic demand cliff. **Lucira/Pfizer** — strategic
abandonment after acquisition. **Forward Health** — burned $650M on AI CarePods that
hard-locked patients inside; charged $150/mo flat, zero unit economics. **uBiome** —
FBI raid over insurance billing fraud (2019). **Arivale** — couldn't make $99/mo
"scientific wellness" pencil, closed 2019. **Pattern**: (1) single-pathogen products
die when pandemic demand fades, (2) hardware QA failure is existential, (3) consumer
subscription economics on physical kits are brutal, (4) insurance billing creativity
= federal investigation, (5) "AI replaces doctor" without insurance reimbursement =
burn out at $400M+.

## 7. White-space opportunities

The defensible wedge for **Smart Nasal Swab** is *not* faster pathogen ID — Visby,
Aptitude, and BIOFIRE already win on that. Three real gaps:

1. **Single-swab "what-and-when-and-what-now"** — combining pathogen identity +
   mucosal IgA/IgG titer (early vs. late infection, prior immunity) + an LLM-driven
   treatment recommendation pipeline (e.g., "Flu A, day 2 of infection, candidate for
   oseltamivir; here's an e-Rx"). No competitor in the table does all three. Adaptive
   is blood + lab. Karius is hospital-only. The new molecular POC players do
   pathogen-only.
2. **Treatment-recommendation + Rx loop, not just a result.** This is where the
   federal "Test-to-Treat" infrastructure (Walmart, CVS, Amazon Clinic) plus EUA-era
   patient-collected nasal swabs creates a regulatory/payer template. The
   differentiator is the AI layer functioning as a credentialed-clinician-facing
   decision-support tool (Class II SaMD path), not patient autonomous prescribing —
   which is where Forward Health died.
3. **Antibody-stage angle as the moat.** Mucosal IgA peaks ~3 weeks post-infection
   per published SARS-CoV-2 literature; no shipping product reads it at home. This is
   the technical defensibility no one else has — pathogen RNA + mucosal antibody from
   one swab is novel claim space, viable Breakthrough Device pathway.

## 8. Funding climate (2024–2026)

Diagnostics-only deals are down — Q3 2025 saw global venture deal count fall ~18%
YoY, with capital concentrating on category leaders. But the *AI + Dx* lane is hot:
**a16z Bio+Health** funded Sprinter Health ($55M, at-home care), and a16z launched a
Lilly-partnered biotech ecosystem fund in 2025. **Khosla Ventures** stayed extremely
active (12 rounds in May 2025 alone) and has direct precedent in this space — they
led Karius's $100M and SiPhox's $27M. **5AM Ventures, Gilde Healthcare, Illumina
Ventures, ARCH, GV, Founders Fund Bio, BARDA (non-dilutive)** are all writing checks
for diagnostic platforms with multi-product roadmaps. The bar is much higher than
2021: investors want sustainable unit economics, regulatory clarity, and a
non-COVID-dependent demand story. A solo-COVID story is unfundable; a multi-pathogen
+ AI + treatment-loop story matches what Visby ($135M) and Karius ($100M) raised.

---

**Key takeaways:** The single-pathogen at-home molecular category is a graveyard
(Cue, Lucira, Ellume all dead/dying); the survivors (Visby, Aptitude, BIOFIRE)
compete on speed/multiplex but none combine pathogen ID + antibody stage + AI
treatment recommendation. The white-space wedge for Smart Nasal Swab is the
**three-in-one swab** (RNA + mucosal IgA + LLM treatment-loop), positioned as Class
II SaMD into the federal Test-to-Treat infrastructure. Funding is selective but
diagnostics+AI is fundable when the story is multi-pathogen and not COVID-dependent.
